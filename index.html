Mountains & Trekking

The mountains are my sanctuary and greatest teacher. Through trekking and wilderness exploration, I connect with the raw beauty and wisdom of nature, finding endless inspiration and a profound sense of perspective that grounds my entire life.

Each trail offers new vistas, challenges, and moments of awe that remind me of the delicate balance between effort and surrender—a principle that guides both my adventures and meditation practice. In the mountains, I practice a walking meditation that attunes me to the rhythms of the natural world and my own breath, creating a seamless connection between inner and outer landscapes.

Some of my favorite treks include:

  • Annapurna Circuit - A classic Himalayan journey through diverse landscapes
  • Everest Base Camp - Walking in the shadow of the world's highest peak
  • Langtang Valley - A less-traveled but equally stunning Himalayan route
  • Manaslu Circuit - Remote trails with incredible cultural experiences

The mountains have taught me patience, resilience, and humility—qualities that serve me equally well in all aspects of life. They remind me that transformation happens gradually, through consistent effort and a willingness to embrace both the summit views and the challenging climbs that lead to them.

Vipassana Meditation

My journey with Vipassana meditation began at Dharma Shringa, a serene meditation center nestled in the hills of Budanilkantha, Nepal. This transformative experience has become the cornerstone of my spiritual practice and has profoundly influenced how I perceive and interact with the world.

The 10-day silent retreat at Dharma Shringa was both the most challenging and rewarding experience of my life. Disconnected from the outside world—no phones, books, writing materials, or communication—I was left with only my mind to observe. The first days were difficult as my mind rebelled against the stillness, churning with thoughts, memories, and plans. But gradually, as I followed the technique of observing sensations throughout my body with equanimity, a remarkable clarity began to emerge.

Vipassana, which means "to see things as they really are," taught me to observe physical sensations without reacting with craving or aversion. This practice revealed how deeply our reactions to pleasant and unpleasant experiences create patterns of suffering. By maintaining equanimity toward all sensations—whether pleasant, unpleasant, or neutral—I began to break these patterns and experience a profound sense of peace.

The daily schedule was intense:

  • 4:00 AM - Morning wake-up bell
  • 4:30-6:30 AM - Meditation in the hall or room
  • 6:30-8:00 AM - Breakfast and rest
  • 8:00-11:00 AM - Group meditation in the hall
  • 11:00-1:00 PM - Lunch and rest
  • 1:00-5:00 PM - Meditation in the hall or room
  • 5:00-6:00 PM - Tea break
  • 6:00-7:00 PM - Group meditation in the hall
  • 7:00-8:15 PM - Teacher's discourse
  • 8:15-9:00 PM - Final group meditation
  • 9:30 PM - Lights out

By the final days, I experienced moments of profound stillness where the boundaries between my body and the surrounding environment seemed to dissolve. The persistent chatter of my mind had quieted, revealing a spacious awareness that felt both ancient and intimately familiar.

Since that first retreat, I've returned to Dharma Shringa several times, each visit deepening my practice. Vipassana has become an essential tool that enhances every aspect of my life—from my mountain treks to my creative work, from my relationships to my understanding of myself. It has taught me that true freedom comes not from controlling external circumstances but from changing how I respond to them.

Traveling

Traveling is more than just visiting new places—it's about immersing myself in different cultures, perspectives, and landscapes. Each journey expands my understanding of the world and deepens my appreciation for the incredible diversity of human experience.

My travels have taken me across continents, from remote mountain villages to bustling urban centers. I'm particularly drawn to destinations that offer a blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and opportunities for adventure. Whether I'm backpacking through Southeast Asia, exploring European cities, or discovering hidden gems in my own country, I approach each journey with curiosity and openness.

Some of my most memorable travel experiences include:

  • Living with local families in remote Himalayan villages
  • Documenting traditional festivals and ceremonies
  • Exploring ancient temples and historical sites
  • Participating in cultural exchanges and volunteer projects

Through travel, I've learned that despite our apparent differences, people everywhere share fundamental desires for connection, meaning, and happiness. These insights continually inform my worldview and enrich my storytelling through cinematography.

Cinematography & Editing

Through the lens of my camera, I capture the stories that words alone cannot tell. Cinematography allows me to document my adventures, preserve fleeting moments, and share the beauty of our world with others.

My approach to filmmaking is deeply influenced by my meditation practice—I strive to bring the same quality of presence and attention to my visual storytelling. Whether I'm filming the majesty of mountain landscapes, the intimate moments of cultural ceremonies, or the dynamic action of sporting events, I seek to convey not just what I see, but how it feels to be there.

The editing process is where the magic truly happens. Through careful selection, pacing, and sound design, I craft narratives that transport viewers and evoke emotional responses. I'm particularly interested in the intersection of natural beauty and human experience—how landscapes shape cultures and how people interact with their environments.

Some of my cinematography projects include:

  • Documentary series on traditional Himalayan cultures
  • Short films about environmental conservation efforts
  • Adventure sports documentaries
  • Time-lapse sequences of astronomical events

Through my films, I hope to inspire others to explore, protect, and appreciate the extraordinary world we inhabit.

Space Science

My fascination with space began as a child, gazing at the stars from mountain viewpoints with unobstructed night skies. This early wonder has evolved into a passionate interest in astronomy and space science that complements my love for terrestrial exploration.

I'm particularly interested in the intersection of astronomy and photography, using specialized equipment to capture celestial events and night sky phenomena. From meteor showers to eclipses, from planetary conjunctions to the Milky Way, I document the cosmic dance that unfolds above us every night.

My interest in space science extends beyond observation to understanding the fundamental principles that govern our universe. I follow developments in astrophysics, planetary science, and space exploration with keen interest, and often incorporate these themes into my cinematography work.

Some of my space-related activities include:

  • Astrophotography expeditions to dark sky locations
  • Time-lapse documentation of celestial events
  • Educational outreach about astronomy and space science
  • Participation in citizen science projects related to astronomy

The cosmic perspective gained through studying space science provides a profound context for all my earthly adventures, reminding me of both our insignificance in the universal scale and the extraordinary privilege of experiencing consciousness in this vast cosmos.

Sports

Physical activity has always been an essential part of my life, complementing my mental and spiritual practices. Through various sports, I've learned valuable lessons about discipline, teamwork, perseverance, and the joy of pushing personal limits.

Cricket holds a special place in my heart. The strategic complexity, the blend of individual skill and team coordination, and the rich traditions of the game continue to fascinate me. Whether playing in local matches or following international tournaments, cricket connects me to a global community of enthusiasts.

Football (soccer) offers a different kind of excitement—the fluid movement, the constant adaptation, and the beautiful simplicity of the game make it both a joy to play and to watch. The universal language of football transcends cultural barriers, creating instant connections wherever I travel.

Swimming provides a meditative counterpoint to these team sports. In the water, I find a unique combination of physical challenge and mental calm. The rhythmic nature of swimming, the sensation of weightlessness, and the focus on breath control create an experience that parallels meditation in many ways.

Through these diverse sporting activities, I maintain physical fitness for my mountain adventures while also enjoying the social connections, competitive spirit, and pure joy of movement that sports provide.

Reading

Books have been my constant companions throughout life's journey, offering wisdom, inspiration, and perspective that continually shape my understanding of the world. While I enjoy various genres, biographies hold a special place in my reading life.

There's something profoundly moving about immersing myself in the life stories of remarkable individuals who have shaped our world. Through biographies, I've walked alongside mountaineers conquering impossible peaks, scientists revolutionizing our understanding of the universe, artists transforming their visions into reality, and leaders navigating complex social and political landscapes.

Some of my favorite biographies include:

  • "Into Thin Air" by Jon Krakauer - A gripping account of the 1996 Mount Everest disaster that offers profound insights into human decision-making under extreme conditions
  • "A Beautiful Mind" by Sylvia Nasar - The fascinating life story of mathematician John Nash and his struggle with schizophrenia
  • "Steve Jobs" by Walter Isaacson - An illuminating portrait of the complex genius behind Apple
  • "Long Walk to Freedom" by Nelson Mandela - The inspiring autobiography of South Africa's revolutionary leader
  • "Becoming" by Michelle Obama - A thoughtful reflection on identity, purpose, and public service
  • "Elon Musk" by Ashlee Vance - The remarkable journey of a visionary entrepreneur reshaping multiple industries

Beyond biographies, I'm drawn to books on philosophy, science, and adventure narratives. Authors like Yuval Noah Harari, Carl Sagan, and Rebecca Solnit have profoundly influenced my thinking about human history, our place in the cosmos, and our relationship with the natural world.

Reading is not just a pastime but a practice—a way of expanding my horizons, challenging my assumptions, and developing empathy by experiencing life through others' perspectives. Whether I'm reading in a quiet corner at home, during breaks on a mountain trek, or listening to audiobooks while traveling, books remain essential companions on my journey of exploration and growth.

Vipassana Meditation

Discovering inner peace at Dharma Shringa, Budanilkantha

My journey with Vipassana meditation began at Dharma Shringa, a serene meditation center nestled in the hills of Budanilkantha, Nepal. This transformative experience has become the cornerstone of my spiritual practice and has profoundly influenced how I perceive and interact with the world.

The 10-day silent retreat was both the most challenging and rewarding experience of my life. Disconnected from the outside world—no phones, books, writing materials, or communication—I was left with only my mind to observe. Through the ancient technique of observing bodily sensations with equanimity, I discovered a profound stillness that continues to guide me through life's adventures.

Vipassana has taught me that true freedom comes not from controlling external circumstances but from changing how I respond to them. This wisdom enhances every aspect of my life—from mountain treks to creative work, from relationships to self-understanding. The practice continues to reveal that the path to peace lies not in escaping life's challenges but in facing them with awareness and equanimity.

The Vipassana Experience

Noble Silence

Maintaining complete silence for 10 days, allowing deep introspection and heightened awareness of sensations.

Anapana Meditation

Focusing on natural breath to develop concentration before learning the Vipassana technique.

Body Scanning

Systematically observing sensations throughout the body with equanimity, without generating craving or aversion.

Metta Bhavana

Cultivating loving-kindness and sharing the merits of practice with all beings on the final day of the retreat.
